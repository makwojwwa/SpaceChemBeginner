Reactor structure


Reactor structure with annotations

The reactor is composed of three parts: the input area to the left (1), the output area to the right (2) and the area between them. In most levels the input and output areas are sub-divided into α and β input areas and ψ and ω output areas, respectively. Atoms/molecules appear in the input areas and must be delivered to the output areas.

Your task is to carry out various reactions with the use of waldos In other words, a remote manipulator. Used inside the reactor for executing the instructions placed along a path. There are two of them: a red and a blue one.. There is a red (3) and a blue (4) one, so they can execute two different (or the same) algorithms at the same time. The goal is to create paths for the waldos with the use of the available instructions so that the required chemical compound can be formed and output in the correct output area.

Below the reactor are buttons with various instructions (5) that you are supposed to use. They are described in detail in Basic instructions. Remember that Advanced instructions (which are not visible in the image above) will be introduced later in the game. The other elements of the interface are described in detail in Reactor interface.

The reactor most often contains also special objects, which are required for carrying out the reaction or executing the algorithm. There are bonders A special object inside the reactor. Used for adding or removing bonds between atoms., sensors A special object inside the reactor. Used for detecting which atom is present above it at a given moment. and fusion laser targets A special object inside the reactor. Used for performing atomic fusion of two atoms to obtain a completely new one.. They are described in detail in Special objects.
